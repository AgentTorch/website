Large Population Models

making complexity simple

Our world is complex - from disease pandemics that spread across continents to global supply chains that connect industries, from the social interactions that shape our communities to the biological processes that govern aging. This complexity persists across scales, emerging from the interactions of millions of individual entities. Large Population Models aims to understand, predict, and shape these complex systems, making the intricate comprehensible. Our mission is to decode the rules governing complexity - from cells to societies - and utilize this understanding to solve the most pressing challenges facing our interconnected world. LPMs will drive evidence-based decisions and accelerate scientific discovery, paving the way for a more informed and sustainable future.

Research pillars

Large Population Models (LPMs) represent the next frontier of decision engines, providing reliable, domain-specific insights by simulating intricate ecosystems with millions of interactions. While Large Language Models (LLMs) excel at representing individual creativity and behavior, LPMs complement them by modeling the complex patterns that emerge when individuals interact at scale. Our key contributions include efficient scaling to millions of agents, robust integration of real-world data, seamless privacy preservation and collaboration, and reliable analysis via auto-differentiation. By combining the strengths of LPMs and LLMs, we enable the creation of high-fidelity digital twins of the real world. LPMs are building the future of decision engines - inside the body, around us, and beyond, revolutionizing the way we understand and navigate complexity.
