Mission

The MIT Media Lab is proud to share the launch of a new, collaborative program ‘Decentralized Society with Web3’ focused on addressing challenges posed by the multi-polar coordination, augmenting the current decentralization technologies, and fostering the joint development of societal applications and commercial solutions.

We are witnessing a tidal wave of new digital technologies well beyond blockchain or AI. The Media Lab’s “Decentralized Society with Web3” program includes three emerging areas of focus.

  • Central Bank Digital Currencies
  • Decentralized AI+Web3
  • Computational Privacy and Security

Our mission is to bring together key leaders from across the globe including technology companies (large and small), local/state/federal governments, and academia/research to share, create, and iterate together as we enter the next phase of highly democratized and pluralistic internet.

Research pillars

Research pillars

01

Security and Privacy

Cryptocurrencies are only pseudonymous, i.e., identities are not linked with their digital transactions. Nonetheless, it has been shown that there are plenty of ways to link people with transactions. Hence, an important challenge moving forward is to address the privacy concerns regarding cryptocurrencies.

02

Central Bank Digital Currencies

Web3 has ushered rapid innovation mainly in the financial sector with the growing popularity of cryptocurrencies, consolidating the demand for digitizing currency. If we wish to use crypocurrency like cash, we must create digital cash that is a liability of the central bank.

03

Decentralized AI + Web3

We propose creating a web3-like system with a distributed artificial intelligence (AI) component. This decentralized system would aggregate data relevant to patients, for example a patient with lingering chest pain after COVID-19, train a model, and predict effective treatments to address the patients’ condition.
